1935–1945: A Decade of Change

Historic Events from a Defining Era

1935 – Nuremberg Laws Enacted

The Nazi regime institutionalizes anti-Jewish laws, setting the stage for systemic persecution.

1939 – World War II Begins

Germany’s invasion of Poland sparks declarations of war by Britain and France.

1941 – Pearl Harbor

Japan launches a surprise attack on the U.S. Pacific Fleet, leading to American entry into the war.

1942 – Executive Order 9066

President Roosevelt authorizes internment of Japanese Americans.

1944 – D-Day Invasion

Allied forces land in Normandy, beginning the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi control.

1945 – End of World War II

Germany surrenders in May; Japan follows in August after the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
